Normal People by Sally Rooney

Genre: Contemporary, Adult Literary Fiction

While it is a Man booker prize long list nominee and is a book that has many admirers, sadly it didn't work for me.
Why? Why do I do this to myself? Sighâ€¦ I get it. This novel is very popular and much loved.
While I recognize that Rooney has talent, I did not vibe with Normal People!
There are characters who dislike themselves, shrug a lot, and say "I don't know" 24/7, which are very deep and realistic while being impossible! I felt the whole character's connection was reduced to sex!


More Bodies Will Fall by Ankush Saikia

Genre: Thriller, Fiction

I am always in the mood for thrillers and so, was intrigued by the premise of this novel.
The backdrop is set in northeast India was just the cherry on the top! Every time I read a book set in India, I learn so much about the rich - both good and bad - history of our country and I am in awe of how much we don't know about our own country! The plot is quite multi-layered and everything and everyone is a suspect!
The mystery in itself is very well thought out - not easy to predict and gradually, all loose ends are tied up.
